## 一篇 7–7.5 分范文

**Essay**

Access to clean water is widely regarded as a fundamental human right, and some people therefore argue that every household should receive water free of charge. While I agree that all people should be guaranteed access to a basic supply of safe water, I do not believe that all domestic water use ought to be provided entirely free.

On the one hand, there are strong reasons why governments should ensure that every home has access to clean water regardless of income. Water is essential for drinking, cooking, washing, and sanitation, and without it people face serious risks to both health and dignity. In this sense, access to a minimum amount of water is not a luxury but a necessity. Charging poor families for every drop they use could result in serious inequality, especially in low-income communities where people may already struggle to pay for other essentials. For this reason, providing a basic quantity of water free of charge is a reasonable and humane policy.

On the other hand, making all household water completely free could create significant problems. First, people tend to value resources less when they do not have to pay for them, which may lead to wasteful consumption. This is particularly concerning in regions where water is scarce. Second, water systems require enormous investment in purification, infrastructure, maintenance, and delivery. If users pay nothing at all, governments would have to bear the full financial burden, which may be unsustainable in the long term. A better solution would be to provide enough water for essential daily needs at no cost, while charging for excessive use such as filling swimming pools or watering large gardens.

In conclusion, I believe that clean water is indeed a basic human right, and every household should be guaranteed a free minimum supply. However, it is neither practical nor desirable for all water consumption to be completely free, and users should pay for non-essential or excessive use.

# 这篇修改后的高分版

Access to clean water is widely regarded as a fundamental human right, and some people therefore argue that every household should receive water free of charge. While I agree that people should be guaranteed access to a basic supply of safe water, I do not believe that all domestic water use ought to be provided entirely free.

On the one hand, there are strong reasons why governments should ensure that everyone has access to a basic amount of clean water. It is essential for drinking, cooking, and sanitation, and without it people face serious risks to both health and dignity. In this sense, access to a minimum amount of water is not a luxury but a necessity. Charging poor families for every drop they use could result in serious inequality, especially in low-income communities where people may already struggle to pay for other essentials. For this reason, providing a basic quantity of water free of charge is a reasonable and humane policy.

On the other hand, making all household water completely free could create significant problems. First, people tend to value resources less when they do not have to pay for them, which may lead to wasteful consumption. This is particularly concerning in regions where water is scarce. Second, water systems require enormous investment in purification, infrastructure, maintenance, and delivery. If users pay nothing at all, the government would have to bear the full financial burden, which may be unsustainable in the long term. A better solution would be to provide enough water for essential needs at no cost, while charging for excessive use such as filling swimming pools or watering large gardens.

In conclusion, I believe that clean water is indeed a basic human right, and every household should be guaranteed a free minimum supply. However, it is neither practical nor desirable for all water consumption to be completely free, and users should pay for non-essential or excessive use.

# 八、最常用的“展开句”模板

# 九、直接可套用的主体段骨架

## 骨架1：正面理由段

> One of the main reasons is that **[理由]**. This is because **[解释]**. As a result, **[结果]**. For example, **[例子]**.

## 骨架2：反面理由段

> However, this approach may also create problems. In many cases, **[问题]**. This can lead to **[后果]**, especially when **[条件]**.

## 骨架3：折中方案段

> A more balanced approach would be to **[方案1]**, while still allowing / requiring **[方案2]**. In this way, **[优点]** without causing **[缺点]**.

# 你现在最适合的训练方式

你不用每天写完整作文。
更高效的方法是：

* 每天拿 1 个题目
* 用这份观点库选 2 个理由
* 只写 **2 个主体段**

这样一个月后，你写 Task 2 会非常稳。
