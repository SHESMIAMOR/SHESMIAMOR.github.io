# Adapting to the Effects of Climate Change

## A

All around the world, nations are already preparing for and adapting to climate change and its impacts. Even if we stopped all CO₂ emissions tomorrow, **[Q15]**, we would continue to see the impact of the CO₂ already released since industrial times, with scientists forecasting that global warming would continue for around **40 years**.

In the meantime, ice caps would continue to melt and sea levels rise. Some countries and regions will suffer more extreme impacts from these changes than others. It's in these places that **innovation is thriving**.

---

## B

In Miami Beach, Florida (USA), seawater isn't just breaching the island city's walls — it is **seeping up through the ground**. As a result, the only way to save the city is to **lift it above sea level**.

Starting in the lowest and most vulnerable neighbourhoods, roads have been raised by as much as **61 centimetres**. **[Q18]**

The elevation work was carried out as part of Miami Beach's ambitious but much-needed **stormwater-management programme**.

In addition to the road adaptations, the city has set up new pumps that can remove up to **75,000 litres of water per minute**. **[Q25]**

In the face of floods, climate-mitigation strategies have often been overlooked, says **Yanira Pineda**, a senior sustainability coordinator.

She emphasises that these strategies are essential and that the job is far from over:

> "We know that in 20, 30, 40 years, we'll need to go back in there and adjust to the changing environment."

---

## C

Seawalls are a staple strategy for many coastal communities. However, on the soft, muddy northern shores of **Java, Indonesia**, they frequently collapse, further exacerbating coastal erosion.

There have been many attempts to restore **[Q14]** the island's **coastal mangroves** — ecosystems of trees and shrubs that defend coastal areas by:

- trapping sediment in their root systems  
- elevating the sea bed  
- dampening the energy of waves and tidal currents

However, **Susanna Tol** of the non-profit organisation **Wetlands International** says that, although popular, **most mangrove-planting projects fail**. **[Q19]**

Instead, Wetlands International introduced a different approach:

- building **semi-permeable dams**
- using **bamboo poles and brushwood**
- mimicking the role of mangrove roots

This creates favourable conditions for mangroves to **grow back naturally**.

The programme has seen moderate success, especially in areas with **less land subsidence**.

Tol explains:

> "Unfortunately, traditional infrastructure is often single-solution focused."

**[Q23]**

> "For long-term success, it's critical that we transition towards multifunctional approaches that embed natural processes and engage local communities."

## D

In September 2018, floodwaters rose across the rice fields of the **Mekong Delta**, but four small houses rose **with them**. **[Q20]**

Homes in this part of **Vietnam** are traditionally built on stilts, but these houses were designed to **float**.

The modifications were made by the **Buoyant Foundation Project**, a non-profit organisation researching **amphibious housing** since 2006.

**[Q26]**

Founder **Elizabeth English** explains:

> "When I started this, climate change was not on the tip of everybody's tongue, but this technology is becoming necessary in places that didn't previously need it."

**[Q16]**

She adds that amphibious housing is **much cheaper** than permanently elevating houses — about **one-third of the cost** of replacing a building's foundations.

It also avoids the problem that taller houses face: **greater wind damage risk**.

Another advantage is that amphibious structures can be **adapted to local cultural needs** and match the types of houses already common in the community.

## E

**Bangladesh** is especially vulnerable to climate change.

- Most of the country lies **less than one metre above sea level**
- **80%** of the land lies on **floodplains**

According to **Raisa Chowdhury** from **ICCO Cooperation**:

> "Almost 35 million people living on the coastal belt of Bangladesh are currently affected by soil and water salinity."

Instead of fighting against this challenge, one project is helping communities **adapt to salt-affected soils**. **[Q21]**

ICCO Cooperation has worked with **10,000 farmers** to cultivate **naturally salt-tolerant crops**, including:

- carrot  
- potato  
- kohlrabi  
- cabbage  
- beetroot  

These crops perform better in salty soil than traditional staples like **rice and wheat**.

Chowdhury describes the visible impact:

> A barren plot of land can be transformed into a **lush green vegetable garden**.

Since the project began, farmers trained in saline agriculture have reported **two to three additional harvests per year**.

## F

**Greg Spotts**, from Los Angeles (LA), is the **chief sustainability officer** of the city's street services department. **[Q22]**

He leads the **Cool Streets LA programme**, which includes:

- planting trees  
- installing a **cool pavement system**

The goal is to reduce the city's average temperature by **1.5°C**. **[Q24]**

Spotts explains:

> "Urban cooling is literally a matter of life and death for our future in LA."

**[Q17]**

Using a **Geographic Information System (GIS)** data-mapping tool, the programme identified streets with low tree canopy cover.

These streets were coated with a **light-grey reflective surface**, which had already been shown to reduce road temperature in Los Angeles by **6°C**.

One street in the **Winnetka neighbourhood** of San Fernando Valley now appears as a pale crescent — the **only cool spot on an otherwise red thermal image** seen from the **International Space Station**.

## Q15

### 概述
题干：询问 **科学家预测气候变化影响还会持续多久**。

### 对应段落
**A 段**

> we would continue to see the impact of the CO2 already released since industrial times, with scientists forecasting that global warming would continue for around **40 years**.

### 答案
A

### 解题思路
本题考查关于 **“气候变化影响持续时间的预测”**。

在 A 段中，作者指出：

即使 **明天停止所有 CO₂ 排放**，由于工业时代以来已经排放的二氧化碳仍然在发挥作用，因此全球变暖的影响不会立即停止。

文中明确给出了科学家的预测：

**全球变暖还会持续约 40 年（around 40 years）**

这正好对应题干中的：

- prediction（预测）
- 时间长度

因此答案为 **A 段**。

### 避坑提醒
不要被其他段落中的时间信息干扰，例如：

- **B 段**提到：20、30、40 年后可能需要再次调整基础设施  
- **D 段**提到：2006 年开始研究

这些时间都不是关于 **气候变化影响持续多久的科学预测**。

只有 **A 段**是在讨论全球变暖持续时间的宏观预测。

## Q16

### 概述
题干：询问 **某种解决方案具有较高性价比（cost-effective）**。

### 对应段落
**D 段**

> It's much cheaper than permanently elevating houses, English explains – about **a third of what it would cost** to completely replace a building's foundations.

### 答案
D

### 解题思路
本题要求找到 **解释某种解决方案为何具有成本优势** 的段落。

D 段介绍了 **Buoyant Foundation Project** 开发的 **浮动房屋（amphibious houses）**。

文中明确指出：

这种方案

- **much cheaper**（便宜得多）
- 成本只有 **彻底更换地基费用的三分之一**

即：

> about a third of what it would cost

通过 **具体成本比例对比**，说明了这种方案的经济优势。

因此，该段正好解释了该方案 **为什么 cost-effective**。

### 避坑提醒
很多考生在找 “cost-effective” 时只会寻找：

- cheap  
- money  
- cost

但题目要求的是 **explanation（解释）**。

D 段不仅说明 **便宜**，还给出了 **成本对比和比例**，完整解释了为什么该方案具有成本优势。

因此答案为 **D 段**。
