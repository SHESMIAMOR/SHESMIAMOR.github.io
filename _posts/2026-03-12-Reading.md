## Passage Title

**Early Exploration of the West of Canada**

# Original Passage

## A

By the mid-1870s, most of Canada's population was in the eastern part of the country. The western part of the country was sparsely populated, with no major cities or rural settlement areas. As a result, the federal government implemented a plan to encourage agricultural settlement in the West. Western rural settlement was desired, as the eastern agricultural land was already nearly filled to capacity and the large expanses of available land in the West offered great agricultural opportunities, including opportunities for Canada to grow its economy.

## B

In an effort to get people to settle in the West and to increase the country's agricultural output, the Canadian government took measures to secure land there. The Dominion of Canada purchased Rupert's Land from the Hudson's Bay Company in 1870. Soon after in 1872, the federal government enacted the Dominion Lands Act, an act that enabled settlers to acquire 160 acres of free land as long as they remained on the land for a minimum of three years, made specific minor improvements to the land, and paid a $10 registration fee at the beginning. The government also created a Mounted Police Force in 1873 and then sent the Mounties west to secure areas for the future settlers. The initiatives implemented by the government worked, attracting settlers not only from Eastern Canada, but also from Europe and the US. The area of Manitoba was an area that particularly attracted an influx of people with the desire to start a new life.

## C

The Canadian government had decided that the sure-fire way to protect the Canadian territory and to eventually achieve the goal of joining British Columbia with the rest of the country was to get large numbers of people to settle in the West. The settling of the West would then make the construction of a transcontinental railway a necessity; once that was established, the West could ship its agricultural products to feed the burgeoning urban industrial areas of the East. In turn, the East could ship its manufactured products to the West, creating a vibrant East-West economy.

## D

During this immigration period, Winnipeg became the metropolis of the West. Its growth resulted from land speculation, a growth in housing, and the federal government's decision in 1881 to make Winnipeg a major stop along the Canadian Pacific Railway (CPR). With this decision, Winnipeg stood at the junction of three transcontinental railway lines which subsequently employed thousands of workers. With its access to transportation, Winnipeg also became the major processor of agricultural products for the surrounding areas.

## E

Most of the people who settled in and around Winnipeg during this period were Protestant English-speaking settlers from Ontario and the British Isles. These settlers established a culture that was based on the British-Ontarian ethos from which they had come. The ethnic homogeneity of the area did not last very long, however, as increasing numbers of foreign immigrants, especially from Austria-Hungary and Ukraine, soon added a new ethnic element to the region. Settling the West with Eastern Canadians and British immigrants provided Canada with a safeguard from the threat of an American takeover, especially after the Minnesota legislature in the US passed a resolution which provided for the annexation of the fertile Red River area, at the time the most important settlement on the Canadian prairies.

## F

Not all of those who settled in Western Canada in the 1880s wished to remain there, especially after the economic depression of the 1870s and 1880s. This depression kept the value of Canada's main exports low, including agriculture, at a time when many other countries were competing for immigrant settlement. Countries including the US, Brazil, Argentina, Australia, and New Zealand were all open to immigrants and were competing with Canada for those immigrants. As a result, many immigrants opted instead to settle the American frontier in the US. These American settlers included many Canadians. Before 1891, Canada was described as "a huge demographic railway station", in which large numbers of people came and went, with departures often outnumbering arrivals.

## G

By 1891, Eastern Canada had its share of large urban areas and the problems associated with large urban areas. Although the wealthy areas of Toronto and Montreal were wired for electricity and telephones, there were also areas in those cities that had slum conditions where livestock ran on the streets and human waste spilled into public areas. Social reformers were quick to encourage some of these impoverished city dwellers to move to the rural areas, where they could enjoy the fresh air and the open spaces, becoming “true Canadians”. Agriculture played an integral role in the Canadian economy in 1891, as one-third of the workforce worked on farms.

## H

In its attempt to get overseas immigrants to settle in Canada, the Canadian government used a number of incentives, including free or cheap land to potential farmers. Through government agencies, Canada offered assisted passage plans, with bonuses and commissions to both agents and settlers. Also, letters sent overseas from settlers already in Canada to friends and family members often served as successful recruiting tools. Many of these letters painted a rosy picture of the new world, as many settlers already in Canada did not want to disappoint or worry family or friends from their homeland. So, embellished tales of wonder, good fortune, and happiness often succeeded in encouraging others to immigrate from their home countries to Canada.

# Wrong Question Analysis

## Q29

### Summary Gap

> where ______ development was encouraged

### My Answer

**E economic**

### Correct Answer

**G farming**

### Evidence from the passage

> the federal government implemented a plan to encourage **agricultural settlement** in the West

### Analysis

这里的核心同义替换是：

- agricultural settlement
- agricultural opportunities

都更直接对应 **farming development**，而不是笼统的 **economic development**。

虽然 A 段也提到了 “grow its economy”，但那是更大的结果，不是这个空格最直接的搭配。

### Core Paraphrase

- agricultural settlement = farming development

### Error Type

- Chose a broader word instead of the precise one
- Did not follow the closest logical match

### Fix

Summary 填空时优先找：

1. 最直接的同义替换
2. 最自然的搭配

不要选“大而泛”的词。

## Q39

### Question

**Canada was once described as 'a huge demographic railway station' because**

### My Answer

**A** its geographic location was convenient for travelers to transfer

### Correct Answer

**D** there was great population loss due to migration mobility

### Evidence from the passage

> Before 1891, Canada was described as "a huge demographic railway station", in which large numbers of people came and went, with departures often outnumbering arrivals.

### Analysis

这题的关键词不是 **railway**，而是 **demographic railway station**。

题目的比喻重点是：

- people came and went
- departures often outnumbered arrivals

所以它描述的是：

**人口流动性很强，而且流失严重**

不是说加拿大真的像交通中转站那样地理方便。

### Core Paraphrase

- came and went = migration mobility
- departures often outnumbered arrivals = population loss

### Error Type

- Misread metaphor literally
- Focused on surface keyword "railway" instead of demographic meaning

### Fix

Multiple choice 遇到比喻时，先问：

> 这个比喻真正说明的是什么？

不是按字面理解。

# Error Pattern Summary

## Main Pattern 1: Summary 题容易把相邻答案填反

Q29 和 Q30 就是典型：

- farming development
- economic growth

你知道大概意思，但没有把两个空各自的精确逻辑分清楚。

---

## Main Pattern 2: 容易忽略词性和搭配

例如 Q32：

- offer 前需要形容词
- immigrants 是名词

如果先判断词性，这题可以直接排除。

---

## Main Pattern 3: 容易被背景词误导

例如：

- explorers
- railway

这些词看起来和文章背景很搭，但不是原文要表达的精确含义。

---

## Main Pattern 4: 对同义替换敏感度还不够稳定

例如：

- purchased = bought
- agricultural settlement = farming development
- grow its economy = economic growth
- influx of people = immigrants

# What I Should Learn from This Passage

## 1. Summary completion needs precision, not just topic understanding

我不能只知道“这段讲农业、经济、移民”，还要知道：

- 哪个空是局部原因
- 哪个空是整体结果
- 哪个词性最合适

---

## 2. TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN 这篇做得不错

这说明我不是完全不会判断题，说明我的阅读逻辑是有基础的。

这篇真正的问题更偏向：

- summary precision
- word matching
- metaphor interpretation

---

## 3. Multiple choice 要抓比喻背后的真实意思

Q39 里：

- railway station 不是交通站
- demographic railway station 是“人口来来去去”

以后不能按字面硬选。

# How to Improve My Accuracy

## 1. For summary completion, check part of speech first

Example:

- ______ development → likely adjective / noun modifier
- ______ offer → likely adjective
- a flood of ______ → likely plural people noun

---

## 2. For nearby blanks, compare them together

比如这篇：

- Q29 = farming development
- Q30 = economic growth

要成对处理，防止互换。

---

## 3. Prefer exact paraphrase over general meaning

Examples:

- purchased → bought
- settlers → immigrants
- agricultural settlement → farming development

---

## 4. For metaphorical options, translate the meaning, not the image

Example:

- huge demographic railway station
- 真正意思：people came and went, departures often outnumbered arrivals
